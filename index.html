Industry Visit Experience


✈️ Day 1: Touchdown in Manila

Our OJT journey officially began with an early morning flight from Zamboanga to Manila. The moment we landed, the excitement kicked in and we were finally stepping into the first leg of our internship adventure. After settling in, we had lunch together to fuel up for the day ahead. By 1:00 PM, we kicked off our Manila tour, starting with one of the most iconic spots in the city which is Rizal Park. It was more than just sightseeing, standing in a place so rich with history reminded us of how far we’ve come and how much we were about to experience.

From there, we continued exploring, taking in the energy of the capital city and preparing ourselves for the busy yet meaningful days ahead.


🌊 Day 2: Exploring Subic

On our second day, we traveled to Subic Bay Freeport Zone, a place known for its mix of natural beauty and industrial progress. The fresh air, clean surroundings, and organized layout of the area made it feel completely different from the busy city life we experienced in Manila. We also visited the Subic Bay Metropolitan Authority (SBMA), where we learned about how the Freeport Zone is managed and maintained. One of the highlights was discovering how technology plays a big role in their operations. SBMA uses various IT systems for logistics, traffic management, port monitoring, and environmental regulation. These innovations help ensure the area runs efficiently, securely, and supporting both local and international businesses. It was inspiring to see how digital tools and strategic planning can come together to transform a former military base into a model of smart governance and development.

This day was both relaxing and eye-opening, showing us how nature, business, and technology can thrive in harmony.


🏛️ Day 3: A Walk Through History

Our third day was all about culture, heritage, and national pride. We started with a visit to the National Museum of Natural History, where we marveled at exhibits showcasing the Philippines' rich biodiversity. From towering tree replicas to preserved native species, it was a fascinating mix of science and storytelling that reminded us how beautiful and unique our country truly is. Next, we headed to the Museo ni Manuel Quezon, where we learned about the life and legacy of one of the most influential presidents in Philippine history. Walking through the halls filled with historical artifacts and personal items gave us a deeper appreciation for his contributions to the nation. To wrap up the day, we explored the Presidential Car Museum, a hidden gem that showcased the official vehicles used by past Philippine presidents. It felt like stepping into a moving timeline, with each car telling its own story and representing different eras of leadership and style.

This day wasn’t just educational. It was inspiring and reminded us of our roots and the leaders who shaped the path we walk on today.


🏭💵 Day 4: Power and Currency

Day 4 was a deep dive into two very different but equally important industries: technology and finance. Our first stop was at Hytec Power Inc., where we were introduced to the company’s role in providing advanced technical and industrial equipment to schools and institutions across the country. We saw how their machines are used for training in engineering and automation, giving students hands-on experience with industry-grade tools. It was exciting to see how technology is being used to improve education and prepare the future workforce. In the afternoon, we visited the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP). This was one of the most fascinating parts of our trip so far. We were given a behind-the-scenes look at how Philippine banknotes are designed and produced, from security features to printing processes. It was impressive to learn how much technology, precision, and care go into making something we use every day.

This day opened our eyes to how power and value, both literally and economically, are built and protected through innovation, discipline, and smart systems.


🚦🚈 Day 5: Systems That Keep the City Moving

Day 5 gave us a closer look at the systems that keep a city as busy as Metro Manila organized and moving. Our first stop was the Metropolitan Manila Development Authority (MMDA). We learned how they manage traffic flow, urban planning, and public safety across the metro. Their command center was especially impressive, equipped with real-time monitoring screens, surveillance feeds, and traffic data analytics. It showed us how technology is used to make quick decisions during emergencies, manage congestion, and coordinate across different government units. Next, we visited the Light Rail Transit (LRT) system. There, we saw how public transportation is secured and maintained. We were introduced to the security protocols in place, such as surveillance systems, access control, and emergency response procedures. We also learned about the technology behind train operations, including signaling systems and power controls.

It was eye-opening to see how coordination, innovation, and technology come together to keep a large city running smoothly and safely


🏞️ Day 6: Baguio City Highlights

Day 6 took us to the cool and scenic landscapes of Baguio City, a refreshing change from the hustle and heat of the lowlands. We began our tour with a visit to the prestigious Philippine Military Academy (PMA). Walking around the campus gave us a glimpse of the discipline, structure, and dedication it takes to serve in the military. The wide open grounds and historical displays made the experience both peaceful and inspiring. Next, we headed to Mines View Park, where we enjoyed breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys. It was the perfect spot for photos, souvenirs, and a bit of local culture. Then we stopped by the Bell Church, a serene and beautifully designed place that reflects the city's Chinese-Filipino heritage. The rest of the day was spent roaming around the city, enjoying the cool breeze, local food, and relaxed atmosphere that Baguio is known for.

It was the perfect way to unwind and appreciate the cultural and natural beauty of Baguio city.


☕🚐 Day 7: A Bittersweet Farewell

Our final day began with a calm and cozy morning in Baguio City. We visited a local café, enjoying hot drinks and light conversations while taking in the chilly breeze one last time. It was the perfect setting to relax, reflect, and soak in the last few hours of our unforgettable journey. In the afternoon, we packed up and began our ride back home to Zamboanga City. The long drive gave us time to rest, revisit our favorite memories, and appreciate everything we had experienced throughout the tour.

Though we were leaving the places behind, we carried home valuable lessons, new friendships, and memories that will last far beyond this OJT journey.
